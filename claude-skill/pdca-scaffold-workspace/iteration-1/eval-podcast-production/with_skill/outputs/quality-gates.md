# Quality Gates — Podcast Production PDCA

## Definition of Done

A cycle is complete when all of the following are true:

1. Audio edited and host has listened to and approved the edited version
2. Transcript spot-checked — no errors in proper nouns, names, or key claims
3. Show notes reviewed and approved by host in final published form (not draft form)
4. All 5 social posts individually approved by host (not batch-approved)
5. Episode uploaded to hosting platform with accurate title, description, and metadata
6. Social posts scheduled or published per the episode's release plan
7. Episode published or scheduled for the correct release date/time

## Common Failure Modes

- **Show notes without editorial pass**: Detect by asking "did the host edit this or just approve it?" If the AI draft went out unchanged, the voice will be inconsistent.
- **Batch social post approval**: Detect by checking whether each post was reviewed individually. "They all look fine" said quickly is a signal.
- **Transcript errors in final content**: Detect by spot-checking guest name, company name, and any statistics in published show notes against the corrected transcript.
- **AI-invented context**: Detect by verifying every factual claim in show notes traces to a specific timestamp in the transcript.

## Gate Escalation

If a quality gate cannot be passed:
1. Identify what specifically failed
2. Return to DO phase to fix it — do not publish with a known quality gap
3. If the publishing deadline is at risk, notify the host and decide together whether to delay or publish with the gap acknowledged
4. Never reduce the editorial standard to meet a deadline — the show's voice is the asset
