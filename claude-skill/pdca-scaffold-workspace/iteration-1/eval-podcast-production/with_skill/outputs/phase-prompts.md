# Phase Prompts — Podcast Production PDCA

---

## PLAN Phase

**Purpose:** Review the recording and define what this episode needs.
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes
**Done when:** Transcript spot-checked, key themes extracted, and host has confirmed the
  editorial angle before any content is drafted

### Steps

1. **Recording review** — What is this episode about? Who is the guest?
   Note: topic, guest name and title, key claims made, memorable quotes, main takeaways.
   Flag any audio issues that need editing attention.

2. **Transcript spot-check** — Review auto-transcription for accuracy on:
   - Guest name and title
   - Company names and product names
   - Technical terms and acronyms
   - Any statistics or specific claims
   Correct errors before extracting content.

3. **Extraction pass** — From the corrected transcript, extract:
   - 3-5 key quotes (verbatim, with timestamp)
   - 3-5 main themes or insights
   - 1-2 memorable moments worth highlighting in social
   Present extraction to host for review before drafting.

4. **Human review gate** — Host reviews the extraction and confirms:
   - The editorial angle for show notes (what story does this episode tell?)
   - Which quotes to feature
   - Any framing guidance before drafting begins

> **STOP:** Do not begin drafting show notes until host has confirmed the editorial angle
> and approved the extraction. This is the gate that prevents inconsistent voice.

---

## DO Phase

**Purpose:** Draft show notes and social posts with mandatory host review at each gate.
**Duration:** 1-2 hours
**Done when:** Host has approved show notes and all 5 social posts in final form

### Before Starting

- [ ] PLAN phase complete — extraction reviewed and angle confirmed by host
- [ ] Audio editing complete (or scheduled — do not gate content production on audio)
- [ ] Scope defined — which platforms, which social post formats

### Execution

1. **Draft show notes** — Using the approved extraction and editorial angle:
   - Open with the episode's main insight or hook (not a generic intro)
   - Include guest bio, 3-5 key takeaways with timestamps, and featured quotes
   - Close with a call to action (subscribe, review, next episode)
   - Write in the host's voice — use the extraction, not your own framing

2. **Draft 5 social posts** — One per platform or format:
   - LinkedIn: insight-forward, professional, 150-200 words
   - Twitter/X: punchy, quote-led or insight-led, 240 chars max
   - Instagram: visual hook first, storytelling, hashtags
   - Newsletter teaser: 2-3 sentences, curiosity-gap structure
   - Short-form (TikTok/Reels script): 30-second spoken hook from a memorable moment
   Each post must be grounded in the transcript — no invented context.

3. **Metadata prep** — Episode title, description (for hosting platform), chapter markers
   if the episode is long enough to warrant them.

### Mandatory Human Gates

- GATE: Before show notes are finalized, present the full draft to the host.
  Host edits to their voice — this is not a "looks good" skim but an editorial pass.
  Show notes are not done until the host has written or approved every paragraph.

- GATE: Before any social post is scheduled or published, present each one individually.
  Host reads and approves each post. Batch approval ("they all look fine") is not valid.

> **STOP:** If AI presents show notes or social posts as ready without individual host
> review, stop immediately. Quality and voice consistency depend on this gate.

## CHECK Phase

**Purpose:** Verify quality and editorial standards before publishing.
**Duration:** 15 minutes

### Verification Checklist

- [ ] Audio edited and host-approved
- [ ] Transcript spot-checked for accuracy on proper nouns and key claims
- [ ] Show notes reviewed and approved by host in final form
- [ ] All 5 social posts individually approved by host
- [ ] Episode metadata accurate (title, guest name, description)
- [ ] No claims or opinions in show notes that weren't in the interview

### Failure Mode Check

- [ ] Show notes approved without a real editorial pass — not present
- [ ] Social posts approved as a batch — not present
- [ ] Transcript errors in final content — not present
- [ ] AI-invented context in any output — not present

### Process Audit

- [ ] Human owned audio editing and all editorial decisions
- [ ] Both mandatory gates were observed (show notes, social posts)
- [ ] All content grounded in this episode's transcript

## ACT Phase

**Purpose:** Retrospect and propose skill refinements.
**Duration:** 10 minutes

### Retrospective

**Episode overview:** Guest, topic, what made this episode distinctive.

**Critical moments:** What required the most editorial work? What did AI get wrong?

**Process insights:**
- Which social posts required the most rewriting? (Signal: AI framing isn't matching the show's voice)
- Did the editorial gate slow things down or catch real problems?
- What shortcuts were tempting this cycle?

**Top 3 actionable insights:**
1. Start doing: [specific practice]
2. Stop doing: [specific shortcut to eliminate]
3. Keep doing: [what to protect]

### Skill Refinement

Review `references/working-agreements.md` and propose specific updates based on the retrospective.
If the show notes required significant rewriting, the AI's drafting instructions may need
more voice guidance. If a social post gate was tempting to skip, the trigger language
may need strengthening.
