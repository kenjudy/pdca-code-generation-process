# Working Agreements — Podcast Production PDCA

**Version:** 1
**Last refined:** 2026-05-05
**Refinement history:** See git log for this file.

## Process Discipline

Editorial control stays with the host. Inconsistency comes from AI generating final copy
without the host reviewing it. This process exists to make AI contribution consistent and
the editorial review fast, not to eliminate it.

### Human Owns (Non-Negotiable)

- Audio editing — deciding what to cut, how to pace segments, and what emphasis to keep
  requires editorial judgment about the guest, the topic, and the audience
- Final show notes content — the host's voice and perspective must be present;
  AI drafts, human edits to final
- Approval of each social post — framing, tone, and accuracy are the host's responsibility
- Publishing — pressing publish on the hosting platform and scheduling social posts
- Any claim or opinion attributed to the host that was not in the interview

### AI Executes (With Review Gates)

- Transcription (or review of auto-transcription for accuracy)
  (human spot-checks before show notes drafting begins)
- Extracting key quotes, moments, and themes from the transcript
  (human reviews the extraction before AI uses it for drafting)
- Drafting show notes from extracted content
  (human edits to final — AI draft is a starting point, not the output)
- Drafting all 5 social posts with platform-appropriate length and format
  (human reads and approves each one individually — not as a batch)
- Uploading audio and episode metadata to the hosting platform after human approval
- Scheduling social posts after human approves each one

### Intervention Triggers

When the AI does any of the following, stop immediately:

- STOP if AI publishes or uploads content to the hosting platform without explicit approval
- STOP if AI presents show notes as "done" without a human editorial pass
- STOP if AI presents all 5 social posts as approved without individual human review
- STOP if AI suggests skipping audio editing because the recording "sounds clean"
- STOP if AI adds context, opinions, or claims not present in the interview transcript
- STOP if AI writes show notes that don't sound like the host's voice and doesn't flag it

### Process Debt to Watch For

- Using AI show notes drafts as final copy after a quick skim rather than a real edit
- Approving social posts as a batch without reading each one individually
- Skipping transcript spot-check and trusting auto-transcription fully
- Compressing the production cycle into one day when the recording was late

## Implementation Approach

- Understand the episode before producing. Read the extraction summary before drafting.
- Minimal scope. Show notes for this episode, not a content strategy refresh.
- One phase at a time. Do not draft show notes before transcript is spot-checked.
- Respond to feedback. If the host rewrites show notes significantly, identify what
  the AI got wrong and note it for refinement.

## Definition of Done

A cycle is complete only when all of the following are true:

- [ ] Audio edited and host-approved (not just rendered)
- [ ] Transcript spot-checked for accuracy on proper nouns and key claims
- [ ] Show notes reviewed and approved by host in final form
- [ ] All 5 social posts individually approved by host
- [ ] Episode uploaded to hosting platform with accurate metadata
- [ ] Social posts scheduled
- [ ] Episode published or scheduled for release
