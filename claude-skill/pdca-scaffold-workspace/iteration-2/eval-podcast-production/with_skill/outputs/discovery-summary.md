# Discovery Summary — Podcast Production PDCA Skill Generation

**Eval iteration:** 2
**Scenario:** B2B SaaS podcast, weekly production
**Date generated:** 2026-05-05
**Method:** Socratic discovery with invented realistic answers (no real human respondent)

---

## Invented Discovery Answers by Layer

### Layer 1 — Task Essence

**Q1 (task + completed cycle):**
Produce one episode of the B2B SaaS podcast per week. A completed cycle delivers a published episode with edited audio, approved show notes, and scheduled LinkedIn and Twitter posts that carry the host's editorial voice.

**Q2 (cycle frequency):**
Weekly, event-triggered by each recorded interview.

---

### Layer 2 — Human-AI Role Division

**Q3 (human judgment required):**
- Selecting the 3-5 key insights to surface from an interview (editorial judgment about what the audience most needs)
- Approving the editorial voice and framing in show notes and social posts (the host's tone is a brand asset)
- Deciding whether an audio segment is usable or too technically flawed to broadcast
- Approving the episode title and description (SEO + brand positioning)
- Final sign-off before any asset is published or scheduled

**Q4 (AI executes with review gates):**
- Transcribing the raw audio interview
- Drafting show notes from the transcript (summary, guest bio pull, key takeaways, links)
- Drafting LinkedIn post and Twitter/X thread from the transcript and approved show notes
- Generating chapter markers and timestamps from the transcript
- Formatting and proofreading the show notes for publication
- Creating a publishing checklist for the episode

**Q5 (irreversible or highest-stakes moments):**
- Publishing the episode audio (cannot unpublish without audience impact)
- Scheduling and sending social posts (once sent, corrections require a follow-up post)
- Sending the guest their quote-approval request (sets expectations externally)

---

### Layer 3 — Quality Contract

**Q6 (done looks like — 5 observable signals):**
1. Edited audio file is approved by the host and uploaded to the podcast host platform
2. Show notes are published to the website, reviewed and approved by the host
3. LinkedIn post is scheduled and approved by the host
4. Twitter/X thread is scheduled and approved by the host
5. Episode is live and the guest has received a thank-you message with the link

**Q7 (common failure modes to prevent):**
- AI-drafted copy that loses the host's voice (generic, listicle-style)
- Publishing without a final host approval step
- Show notes that omit or misattribute a key insight from the guest
- Social posts that make claims the guest did not actually make
- Audio going out with a technical flaw the host did not notice

---

### Layer 4 — Process Shape

**Q8 (phase boundaries):**
- Analysis done: Host has reviewed the raw transcript, selected 3-5 key insights, confirmed the episode angle/framing, and approved the production plan. AI has all inputs needed to begin drafting.
- Execution done: All draft assets exist (show notes, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, chapter markers), each has been through one AI revision pass, and the host has a complete review package assembled.

**Q9 (STOP triggers):**
- AI publishes or schedules any asset without explicit host approval
- AI drafts social content that includes a direct quote not verbatim from the transcript
- AI selects key insights without host confirmation of the framing
- AI expands scope to additional social platforms or content types not in the approved plan
- AI proceeds to drafting before the transcript review and insight-selection gate is complete

---

### Layer 5 — Learning Loop

**Q10 (process debt that accumulates):**
- Skipping the transcript review to "save time" and going straight to drafting
- Reusing a social post template so often that every episode sounds identical
- Host approvals becoming rubber stamps (approving without actually reading)
- Letting audio quality bar drift downward over time
- Not updating the show notes template when the podcast format evolves

**Q11 (what to learn and improve each cycle):**
- Which parts of the host's approval feedback were predictable (catch it earlier)
- Whether AI drafts are landing closer to the host's voice over time
- Where the production bottleneck actually sits (transcript, drafts, or approval)
- Guest satisfaction signals (do they share? do they object to anything?)

## Confirmation Summary (as presented before generation)

**Task:** Produce one weekly B2B SaaS podcast episode delivering edited audio, approved show notes, and scheduled LinkedIn/Twitter posts that carry the host's editorial voice.
**Cycle frequency:** Weekly, event-triggered by each recorded interview.

**Human owns:** Insight selection, voice/tone approval on all written assets, audio quality decisions, episode title/description, final sign-off on all assets, guest quote-approval outreach.

**AI executes:** Transcription, show notes drafting, social post drafting, chapter markers, formatting/proofreading, publishing checklist — all with host review gates before any asset proceeds.

**Done looks like:** Audio uploaded and approved; show notes published and approved; LinkedIn scheduled and approved; Twitter/X scheduled and approved; episode live and guest thanked.

**STOP triggers:** Scheduling/publishing without approval; non-verbatim quotes in social; AI-selected insights without host confirmation; scope expansion; drafting before insight-selection gate.

**Learning loop:** Voice drift tracking, bottleneck location, predictable-feedback catch rate, guest satisfaction signals.

## Notes on Invented Realism

The answers above were designed to reflect a realistic B2B SaaS podcast operation:
- Weekly cadence matches typical interview podcast rhythm
- Editorial voice concern is the dominant real-world tension in AI-assisted podcast production
- Irreversible moments (social posts, audio publishing) reflect actual production risk
- Process debt items (rubber-stamp approvals, template drift) reflect documented patterns in content production workflows
- Guest quote accuracy is a genuine legal and reputational concern that warranted a specific STOP trigger
